How I Work

Every site I build is custom, not just visually, but structurally. That means the design, layout and code are all shaped around the purpose of the project instead of being forced into a prebuilt system.

I focus on:

  • Semantic, accessible HTML
  • Responsive layouts that actually adapt, not just shrink
  • Performance and load speed
  • Clean, maintainable code
  • Designs that prioritize clarity over gimmicks

The result is a site that's easier to update, easier to reason about, and less likely to need a full rebuild a year down the line. I offer guidance when it's useful, especially when something can be simplified or made more durable, but the direction of the project always comes from your goals and not a formula.

My Process

Every project starts with a conversation about your goals, needs, and vision. From there, I design and build the site, keeping you informed throughout. Once complete, we review together and make any final adjustments. The site launches when you're satisfied with the result.

Why Custom Matters

Most website builders optimize for speed of setup, not long-term use. They work well until they don't — when content grows, needs change or the structure starts fighting you. Building from scratch avoids those compromises. You're not locked into a platform, paying for features you don't use or inheriting decisions you didn't make. The site exists to serve the project, not the other way around.

If you want something quick and disposable, there are plenty of tools that do that well. Website builders bundle convenience into a recurring subscription, while a custom site separates costs — standard hosting plus changes only when they're actually needed. If you want a site that's considered, flexible and built to hold up, custom is the right approach.
